Frequently Asked Questions

Cyberbullying refers to the use of digital platforms, such as social media, messaging apps, gaming platforms, or emails, to harass, intimidate, or harm others. It involves repeated and intentional behavior aimed at upsetting or embarrassing someone through online means.
You might be experiencing cyberbullying if you feel distressed or uncomfortable due to messages, posts, or interactions online. Indicators include:
  • Receiving threatening or harmful messages.
  • Being excluded or ostracized from online groups or activities.
  • Seeing embarrassing or offensive content posted about you.
Our model is designed to analyze comments and predict whether they are instances of cyberbullying. It processes each comment and classifies it into different categories, such as threat, obscene, insult, hate speech, severe toxic, and others, based on the severity and nature of the content. The model utilizes advanced natural language processing (NLP) techniques, such as tokenization and lemmatization, combined with machine learning algorithms, to identify harmful patterns in the text. This allows it to effectively flag potentially harmful comments and categorize them into specific types of cyberbullying for further analysis or intervention.
To report cyberbullying, follow these steps:
  1. Save evidence: Take screenshots or save messages as proof.
  2. Block the perpetrator: Use platform tools to block the individual.
  3. Report to the platform: Most social media and online services have reporting mechanisms.
  4. Seek help: Inform trusted individuals, schools, or authorities about the situation.
To prevent cyberbullying:
  • Keep personal information private.
  • Think before you post or share anything online.
  • Use strong and secure passwords for online accounts.
  • Educate yourself and others about online etiquette and the impact of harmful behavior.
  • Report any suspicious or inappropriate behavior online to the respective platforms or authorities.
Cyberbullying can lead to legal consequences, including criminal charges depending on the severity of the actions. Laws may include harassment, defamation, and even stalking. In many jurisdictions, cyberbullying is considered a form of bullying that can lead to fines or imprisonment. Victims may also have the right to file civil suits against perpetrators.
If you are experiencing cyberbullying, you can report it to various platforms or organizations. Some resources include:
  • Cyberbullying Research Center
  • StopBullying.gov
  • Report on social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, etc. Most platforms offer reporting tools for inappropriate behavior.
Here are some helplines you can contact if you or someone you know is experiencing cyberbullying:
  • National Helpline for Cyberbullying (USA): 1-800-273-TALK
  • Cyberbullying Hotline (India): 1098
